One of the most common questions we get: "Should I fix this thing or just buy a new one?"

It's a fair question, and the honest answer is — it depends. Here's how we think about it.

## When Repair Makes Sense

**Your computer is less than 5 years old** and the issue is specific: a cracked screen, failing hard drive, bad battery, or software problem. These are all fixable at a fraction of the replacement cost.

**Common repairs that are almost always worth it:**
- Replacing a hard drive with an SSD (this alone can make an old computer feel new)
- Virus/malware removal and cleanup
- RAM upgrades
- Battery replacement on laptops
- Fan cleaning and thermal paste replacement for overheating issues

**The rule of thumb:** If the repair costs less than 40% of what a comparable new computer would cost, repair is usually the way to go.

## When Replacement Makes Sense

**Your computer is 7+ years old** and experiencing multiple issues. At that point, you're often fighting a losing battle — fix one thing and something else fails next month.

**Signs it's time to replace:**
- The computer can't run the current version of its operating system
- It takes more than 2 minutes to boot up (even after optimization)
- Multiple hardware components are failing
- It can't handle basic tasks like web browsing and email without lag

## The Gray Zone: 5-7 Years Old

This is where it gets tricky. A 6-year-old computer with a single fixable issue? Probably worth repairing. A 6-year-old computer that's slow, has a failing hard drive, AND needs a new battery? That's adding up.
