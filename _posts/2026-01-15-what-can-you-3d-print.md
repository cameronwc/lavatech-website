When most people hear "3D printing," they think of little plastic trinkets or maybe something from a sci-fi movie. But the reality in 2026 is a lot more practical — and a lot more useful — than most people realize.

Here are some of the things we've printed for customers right here in Lava Hot Springs.

## Replacement Parts

This is the number one use case, and it's a game-changer for a small town. When a specific plastic part breaks on an appliance, tool, or piece of equipment, you used to have two options: order the part online (if you can find it) and wait days or weeks, or replace the whole thing.

Now there's a third option: we can often design and print the replacement part the same day.

**Real examples we've done:**
- Refrigerator shelf clips that the manufacturer doesn't sell separately
- Custom brackets for mounting equipment
- Replacement knobs, handles, and covers
- Parts for vintage equipment that's long out of production

## Prototyping for Local Businesses

Got an idea for a product or a custom tool for your business? Before committing to expensive manufacturing, 3D printing lets you test the design in your hands. We've helped local businesses prototype everything from display stands to custom jigs for their workshops.

## Custom Solutions

Sometimes you need a thing that simply doesn't exist. A phone mount that fits a specific spot on your dashboard. A custom bracket to hold a camera at just the right angle. An adapter that connects two things that were never meant to connect.

That's where 3D printing shines — solving problems with custom one-off solutions.

## How It Works

1. **Tell us what you need** — describe the problem, bring in the broken part, or show us a sketch
2. **We design it** — using CAD software, we create a 3D model (or you can bring your own)
3. **We print it** — typically ready within 24-48 hours, depending on size and complexity
4. **We iterate if needed** — the beauty of 3D printing is that adjustments are easy and cheap

## What We Can't Print (Yet)

To be fair, 3D printing has limits. We primarily work with PLA and PETG plastics, which are strong and versatile but not suitable for every application. Very high-heat, high-stress, or food-contact applications may need different solutions.

We'll always be upfront about what's possible and what isn't.
